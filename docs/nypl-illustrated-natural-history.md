# NYPL Illustrated Natural History & Decorative Arts

A guide to the most visually striking collections in the NYPL public domain dataset. All items are pre-1900 and freely reusable (no known US copyright).

Image URLs use the legacy `images.nypl.org` format from the 2016 dump. For hi-res IIIF access, use:

```
https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/{uuid}
```

## Botanical Illustration

### Les Liliacées — Pierre-Joseph Redouté (1805-1816)

495 plates. Redouté was the "Raphael of flowers" — botanical painter to Marie Antoinette and Empress Joséphine. These are lily-family plants (lilies, irises, agaves, aloes) rendered in stipple engraving with watercolor finishing. Considered among the finest botanical illustrations ever made.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/dc28c570-c5fa-012f-a0fe-58d385a7bc34)
- [Agapanthus umbellatus](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/95d368b0-c5fb-012f-f016-58d385a7bc34)
- [Abama ossifraga](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/419ec870-c5fb-012f-8157-58d385a7bc34)

### Hortus Romanus — Giorgio Bonelli (1772-1793)

713 plates. An encyclopedic catalogue of plants in the Roman botanical garden, hand-colored engravings with Latin and Italian names. The largest botanical collection in the dataset.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/7446a6c0-c5f0-012f-41c0-58d385a7bc34)
- [Absinthium Ponticum (Wormwood)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/85868420-c5f1-012f-ffc3-58d385a7bc34)
- [Abutilon crispum (Indian Mallow)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/8948e0f0-c5f0-012f-7e9d-58d385a7bc34)

### Nouveau Duhamel — Traité des arbres (1801-1819)

502 plates of trees and shrubs cultivated in France. Continuation of Duhamel du Monceau's 18th-century tree encyclopedia. Detailed botanical plates with fruit, leaf, and flower details.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/502b1f30-c5fa-012f-3a2c-58d385a7bc34)
- [Ilex Maderiensis (Holly)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/bdef94e0-c5f9-012f-3530-58d385a7bc34)
- [Daphne Cneorum (Garland Flower)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/c135c210-c5f9-012f-c87e-58d385a7bc34)

### Reichenbachia — Orchids Illustrated, F. Sander (1888-1894)

196 chromolithograph plates of orchids. Sander was the "Orchid King" of Victorian England. These are lavish, large-format color illustrations commissioned to showcase his nursery's rarest specimens.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/43d880b0-c643-012f-9f3c-58d385a7bc34)
- [Aeranthus sesquipedalis](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/49d26000-c643-012f-5957-58d385a7bc34)
- [Oncidium jonesianum](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/4c6b4100-c643-012f-b4bb-58d385a7bc34)

## Ornithology

### The Birds of America — John James Audubon (1840-1844)

506 plates. The octavo (smaller) edition of Audubon's legendary work, lithographed by J.T. Bowen. Every North American bird species known at the time, depicted life-size or near life-size in naturalistic settings.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/f48074f0-c5f8-012f-9b20-58d385a7bc34)
- [American Anhinga Snake Bird](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/9baef650-c5f9-012f-6d54-58d385a7bc34)
- [American Dipper](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/290c4c90-c5f9-012f-f3f7-58d385a7bc34)

### The Birds of Great Britain — John Gould (1862-1873)

382 plates. Gould was Audubon's British counterpart. Hand-colored lithographs of every British bird species, drawn by Gould and his wife Elizabeth (one of the great uncredited scientific illustrators).

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/77ba9d00-c606-012f-2348-58d385a7bc34)
- [Alpine Accentor](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/9daa5180-c606-012f-45e5-58d385a7bc34)
- [Sparrow-Hawk](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/7cf61260-c606-012f-727c-58d385a7bc34)

## Ichthyology & Zoology

### Allgemeine Naturgeschichte der Fische / Ichtyologie — Marcus Elieser Bloch (1785-1797)

456 plates. The first comprehensive illustrated encyclopedia of fish. Hand-colored copperplate engravings. Bloch was a Berlin physician who spent 30 years cataloguing every fish species he could find — many are still the type specimens for their species today.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/46ad2ae0-c5fe-012f-66d0-58d385a7bc34)
- [Sea Dragon & Seahorse](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/72e5b130-c5fe-012f-1dee-58d385a7bc34)
- [River Bullhead](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/56dcc3b0-c5fe-012f-4d8b-58d385a7bc34)

### The Fishes of North America — William C. Harris (1898)

44 color plates. Sportfishing guide with beautiful chromolithographs of game fish — trout, bass, salmon — in their natural habitats.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/3adf7ea0-c6cd-012f-ddb7-58d385a7bc34)
- [Hybrid Trout](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/3da24620-c6cd-012f-8ac0-58d385a7bc34)

### Zoology of New York — James E. De Kay (1842-1844)

336 plates. Official state natural history survey. Mammals, birds, reptiles, fish, and invertebrates of New York, illustrated in hand-colored lithographs.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/adb54360-c60d-012f-bcfa-58d385a7bc34)

### Illustrations of Indian Zoology — John Edward Gray (1830-1834)

210 plates. Animals of the Indian subcontinent from Major-General Thomas Hardwicke's collection. Frogs, fish, birds, insects, and ammonites in vivid hand-colored lithographs.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/ab01a7d0-c63d-012f-07ea-58d385a7bc34)

### Zoological Lectures — George Shaw (1806-1809)

169 plates. Lectures delivered at the Royal Institution. Sea anemones, corals, mollusks, and other marine creatures — some of the earliest color illustrations of invertebrate zoology.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/885cfc80-c6b7-012f-c2a6-58d385a7bc34)
- [Actinia (Sea Anemones)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/b4370670-c6b7-012f-86f8-58d385a7bc34)

## Early Photography

### Photographs of British Algae: Cyanotype Impressions — Anna Atkins (1843-1853)

285 cyanotype prints. **The first book illustrated entirely with photographic images.** Atkins placed algae specimens directly onto photosensitive paper and exposed them to sunlight, creating white-on-blue photograms. Each print is unique. This is one of the most important objects in the history of photography.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/28d304b0-c612-012f-cd39-58d385a7bc34)
- [Alaria esculenta](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/75206fb0-c612-012f-ebbc-58d385a7bc34)
- [Asperococcus Turneri](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/46c1e8c0-c612-012f-57d0-58d385a7bc34)

## Art Nouveau / Decorative Design

### La plante et ses applications ornementales — Eugène Grasset (c. 1896)

74 plates. Grasset was a key figure in Art Nouveau. Each plate takes a single plant and transforms it into decorative patterns — wallpaper, tiles, fabric, stained glass. The bridge between botanical illustration and applied design.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/84fe2cd0-c6e4-012f-1676-3c075448cc4b)
- [Aconit (Monkshood)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/7ec40bc0-c6e5-012f-174a-3c075448cc4b)

### L'animal dans la décoration — M. P. Verneuil (1897)

62 plates. The animal counterpart to Grasset's plant book. Eagles, fish, peacocks, and insects rendered as Art Nouveau decorative motifs — wallpaper, friezes, panels.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/758a59b0-c6d8-012f-bb61-3c075448cc4b)
- [Eagles and Oak (wallpaper design)](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/ea45aac0-c6d8-012f-688b-3c075448cc4b)

## Archaeological / Travel Illustration

### L'Art arabe d'après les monuments du Kaire (1877-)

212 plates. Detailed chromolithographs of Islamic architecture and decorative arts in Cairo — geometric tile patterns, arabesques, mosque interiors, carved woodwork. A landmark survey of Islamic decorative art.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/ea7ea7e0-c641-012f-5c0d-58d385a7bc34)

### Arabische und Alt-Italienische Bau-Verzierungen

122 plates. Comparative study of Arab and early Italian architectural ornament — showing the transmission of Islamic geometric patterns into Italian medieval architecture.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/f6a39450-c6ca-012f-e95d-58d385a7bc34)

### Description de l'Égypte (1809-1828)

903 plates. The monumental publication from Napoleon's Egyptian expedition — the work that launched Egyptology. Temples, hieroglyphics, landscapes, natural history, and engineering diagrams.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/47e63850-c5ef-012f-fd62-58d385a7bc34)

### Denkmaeler aus Aegypten und Aethiopien — Karl Richard Lepsius (1849-1856)

932 plates. Lepsius's expedition produced the most comprehensive visual record of Egyptian and Nubian monuments before photography. Color reproductions of tomb paintings, hieroglyphic inscriptions, and architectural details.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/e0ee6e60-c5ef-012f-7e1d-58d385a7bc34)

### Voyage en Perse — Flandin & Coste (1851-1854)

346 plates. Architectural drawings and landscape views from Persia (Iran) — Persepolis, Isfahan, Shiraz. The first systematic visual survey of Persian architecture and archaeology.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/abf1dd70-c641-012f-6c0a-58d385a7bc34)

### Monument de Ninive — P.E. Botta (1849-1850)

398 plates. The excavation of ancient Nineveh (Assyria). Detailed engravings of the colossal winged bulls, bas-reliefs, and cuneiform inscriptions discovered at Khorsabad.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/e2c51ff0-c5ef-012f-2744-58d385a7bc34)

## Indian Colonial Photography & Art

### The People of India (1868-1875)

486 photographs. Early ethnographic photography documenting people across the Indian subcontinent — castes, occupations, regional dress. Historically complicated but visually remarkable as one of the largest 19th-century photographic surveys.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/b6c5b3c0-c63d-012f-4524-58d385a7bc34)

### Indian Coloured Drawings

135 paintings on talc and paper. Indian miniature paintings — Mughal, Rajput, and Company School styles. Delicate gouache and watercolor on mica.

- [Browse collection](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/66c7e280-c63d-012f-ccc3-58d385a7bc34)

## Querying the Dataset

All of these are in `output/nypl.duckdb`. Example queries:

```sql
-- All Audubon plates
SELECT title, uuid FROM nypl_objects
WHERE collection_uuid = 'f48074f0-c5f8-012f-9b20-58d385a7bc34'
ORDER BY title;

-- All botanical collections
SELECT DISTINCT collection_title, count(*) as n
FROM nypl_objects
WHERE classification ILIKE '%botan%'
   OR collection_title ILIKE '%plant%'
   OR collection_title ILIKE '%flor%'
   OR collection_title ILIKE '%hortus%'
GROUP BY collection_title ORDER BY n DESC;

-- Items with the most captures (multi-page illustrated books)
SELECT title, collection_title, num_captures, uuid
FROM nypl_objects
ORDER BY num_captures DESC
LIMIT 20;
```
